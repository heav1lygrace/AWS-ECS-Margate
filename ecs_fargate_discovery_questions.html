ECS Fargate Discovery Questions (Business + Architecture)

Use this question set to understand intended ECS Fargate usage, connectivity, exposure profile selection (A/B/C), and security/operational requirements. You don’t need to ask everything in one meeting—treat this as a structured intake.

Quick profile cue: A = Internet-facing Web (HTTP/HTTPS via ALB + WAF), B = Internal-only, C = Internet-facing Non-Web (TCP/UDP via NLB).

1 Business Context (Why does this exist?)

  1. What business capability does this service provide?
  2. Who are the users/consumers of this service (customers, partners, internal systems)?
  3. Is this service customer-facing, revenue-impacting, or internal support?
  4. What is the business impact if this service is unavailable for 15 minutes, 1 hour, and 1 day?
  5. Are there contractual, regulatory, or data sensitivity requirements (PII/PCI/etc.)?

2 Exposure & Access Model (Determines Profile)

  1. Does this service need to be reachable from the public internet?
  2. If yes, what protocol does it use (HTTP/HTTPS vs TCP/UDP non-web)?
  3. Who must be able to access it (anyone, known IP ranges, specific networks/partners)?
  4. Is there a strong reason this cannot be internal-only?
  5. Can access be restricted geographically or by source?

3 Application Architecture (What runs inside Fargate?)

  1. Is this a long-running service or a batch/worker process?
  2. How many containers per task (single vs sidecars)?
  3. Does the application require persistent storage or only temporary scratch space?
  4. Is the application stateless or stateful?
  5. What happens if a task restarts unexpectedly (data loss, retries, idempotency)?

4 Connectivity & Dependencies (Hidden risk)

  1. What does this service talk to (databases, other services, third-party APIs)?
  2. Are dependencies internal (same VPC/AWS) or external (internet)?
  3. Does it require outbound internet access at all?
  4. Can AWS VPC endpoints be used instead of NAT/internet for AWS services?
  5. Are there hard dependencies that could cause cascading failure?

5 Authentication & Identity (Spoofing risk)

  1. How do clients authenticate to this service (JWT/OAuth, mTLS, API keys, IAM)?
  2. Is authentication handled at the edge/load balancer or in the application?
  3. How are credentials/secrets managed today?
  4. How often are credentials rotated?
  5. Is service-to-service authentication required?

6 Data Sensitivity & Handling (Information disclosure)

  1. What type of data does this service process or store?
  2. Is any sensitive data logged?
  3. Is encryption required in transit and at rest?
  4. Where is data persisted (if at all)?
  5. Are there data residency or retention requirements?

7 Availability, Scale & Failure Modes (DoS & resilience)

  1. What is the expected traffic pattern (steady, bursty, unpredictable)?
  2. Is auto-scaling required?
  3. What is the maximum acceptable scale (cost guardrails)?
  4. What happens if a dependency is slow or unavailable?
  5. Is graceful degradation acceptable?

8 Observability & Operations (Repudiation & response)

  1. What logs are required for troubleshooting and audits?
  2. What metrics indicate the service is unhealthy?
  3. Who receives alerts when something breaks?
  4. Is there an on-call rotation?
  5. What is the incident response expectation?

9 Deployment & Change Model (Tampering risk)

  1. How is the service built and deployed today?
  2. Is CI/CD already in place?
  3. How often do deployments occur?
  4. Are rollbacks automated?
  5. Who is allowed to deploy to production?

10 Governance & Exceptions

  1. Which exposure profile do you believe applies (A / B / C)?
  2. Are you requesting any exceptions to the baseline?
  3. If yes, what is the business justification?
  4. What compensating controls can be applied?
  5. Who owns the risk?
Tip: Run discovery in 2–3 short sessions instead of one long meeting. Start with sections 1–3, then 4–7, and finish with 8–10 for operations and governance.
